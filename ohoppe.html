The presence of veteran catchers and the 2025 transition to Sal Fasano further muddled O’Hoppe’s development, mirroring the mishandling of Nolan Schanuel.

While Molina’s return could help restore O’Hoppe’s natural instincts, Fasano and Travis d’Arnaud may still aid his growth if the coaching message is simplified and unified.

Streamline coaching by blending Fasano’s aggressive style with Molina’s intuitive game-calling. Tailor O’Hoppe’s sequencing to support Detmers’ slider-first strategy. Avoid analytics overload to preserve O’Hoppe’s strengths — much like what’s now helping fuel Schanuel’s 2025 rebound.

Big Picture: What This Really Means

At its core, this theory critiques GM Perry Minasian’s obsession with analytics — not analytics themselves, but the way they’ve been used to override instinct and player development. By replacing feel with formula, the Angels have created confusion behind the plate and regression on the mound.

O’Hoppe, Detmers, and Schanuel are just the clearest examples of a deeper organizational problem: a system that strips away baseball instincts in favor of a one-size-fits-all data model.
