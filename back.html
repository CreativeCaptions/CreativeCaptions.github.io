Who Turned Their Back First?

Some people hesitate to follow the God of the Holy Bible because they don’t want to "turn their back" on the traditions or gods their ancestors followed. They fear looking like a traitor to their heritage.

But what if it was your ancestors who turned their back first?

According to the Bible, the genealogy from Adam to Noah’s son Shem shows a direct line of people who knew and followed the true God. Shem was on the Ark with Noah and lived for centuries after the Flood — more than 500 years of opportunity to pass on the knowledge of God to future generations.

If your ancestors eventually followed other gods, it didn’t start that way. They had access to the truth through Noah and Shem. So someone down the line — not you — turned away first.

Even more interesting: Shem and his brothers were on the Ark not necessarily because of their own righteousness, but because they believed Noah. Genesis 7:1 says God saved Noah’s family because of Noah’s righteousness. That grace gave Shem a front-row seat to God’s power and mercy — and from there, he seems to have embraced it. Noah later blessed Shem and acknowledged his connection to the Lord.

So the real “turncoats” weren’t those who returned to God — they were the ones who abandoned Him after knowing the truth.

Before you reject the God of the Bible based on your ancestors’ choices, ask yourself:

Who turned their back first?

And don’t let their mistake become your own. Return to the God your earliest ancestors knew — the God of the Holy Bible.
