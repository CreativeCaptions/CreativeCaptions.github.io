Per Grok

Short answer: No — you cannot reasonably rely on Mike Trout to play left field with any consistency; DH is the only realistic role to maximize games played.

Context / key facts

Mike Trout is 34 years old (turned 34 in August 2025). Recent, relevant injury timeline:

  • 2021: Right calf strain (missed ~4 months; limited games)
  • 2022: Left costovertebral joint dysfunction — chronic back spasms; missed final 2+ months
  • 2023: Hamate fracture in hand — ~2 months missed
  • 2024: Left meniscus tear (April) → surgery → attempted comeback in July → recurring issues → second surgery → season ended after 29 games
  • 2025 (so far): No outfield work in real games since the 2024 knee injury; limited to rehab/DH activity

Why left field still matters (physically)

  • Sprinting, sudden deceleration, lateral cuts and planting — all common in left field.
  • Long throws and rotational torque stress the core and lower half.
  • Chronic back spasms and a re-torn meniscus flare under repetitive high-impact loading and rotation.

Conclusion: Trout’s history of repeat lower-body and core injuries makes regular outfield duty (even LF) too high risk. The expected roster benefit from a few defensive runs saved does not justify the much larger downside of extended IL time. DH preserves the best chance to get ~120–130 games (optimistic) versus 40–60 if he returns as an OF.
