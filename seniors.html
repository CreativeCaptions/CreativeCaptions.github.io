America Can End Homelessness for Everyone 62+ in Five Years

A focused, five-year federal program to give seniors stable homes, dignity, and community.

The Problem

620,000+ Americans aged 62+ are homeless or one crisis from the street.

Ages 62–64 are the danger zone: too young for Medicare; one medical bill can wipe out housing, savings, and health coverage all at once.

  • Too many seniors are invisible — living in cars, shelters, or doubled-up with unstable families.
  • One crisis (medical, repair, or unexpected bill) often triggers a lifetime of instability.

America moves fast when it decides to

  • Built 300,000 aircraft in four years (WWII)
  • Constructed a 42,000-mile Interstate Highway System in one generation
  • Sent men to the moon in under ten years
  • Created, manufactured, and distributed COVID vaccines in under a year (Operation Warp Speed)

Housing 620,000 seniors is trivial compared to any of those.

The Solution

One five-year program. Estimated total cost: $56–68 billion.

The American Senior Homes Act — “Dignity Villages”

  1. The National Leadership Pledge (annually until complete)
    • Contributors commit the equivalent of a 1% annual wealth stewardship pledge.
    • The pledge can be fulfilled by:
      • Paying it as a surcharge to the IRS, or
      • Directing the same amount — with a 100% tax credit — to the National Senior Housing Trust.
    • Naming Rights for Innovation: Contributors receive long-term naming and branding rights for the communities they fund, inspiring excellence in design, innovation, and maintenance.
  2. Real homes in real neighborhoods (50/50 split)
    • Rehab vacant, abandoned, and blighted properties in existing towns and cities.
    • Quick-build 400–600 sq ft accessible units on those sites and on under-used federal land inside real communities — no remote camps.
  3. Lifetime lock
    • Rent + utilities + tax capped at $500/month, sliding down to $0 as needed.
    • Homes stay in the program forever — no resale, no inheritance, no flipping.
    • Residents are safe for life.
    • No work tests. Seniors have already done their part.

The Math

The top 100 billionaires could fund the entire program with a single 1% of their net worth and still remain richer than 99.999% of humanity the next morning.

The Upside

  • Rebuilt neighborhoods
  • Safer, more stable streets
  • A strengthened local tax base
  • Zero new taxes on the middle class
  • Zero hidden elderly
  • Jobs for those building and maintaining them
  • Technology and services grow to support communities

We pulled off Warp Speed in months. We can give our parents and grandparents a front door in five.

Call to action

Create the legacy leaderboard. Let them inspire each other. Let them lead.

Five years. Done. Dignity restored. And America remembers how to finish what it starts.
