Final Thought
With so few viable assets left, Perry Minasian’s tendency to make quantity-over-quality trades—where the return is too often a parade of fringe MLB talent—should raise concern. The Marsh-for-O’Hoppe deal was a rare win. Most others? AAAA players, overhyped prospects, or expiring contracts dumped on waivers weeks later.

If the Angels are going to rebuild properly—or even try to stay competitive—they can’t afford many more “rose-colored” deals. Minasian needs to go before the Angels make any more deals.
